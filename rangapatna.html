Ranganathaswamy Temple, Srirangapatna



The Ranganthaswamy temple (usually referred to as "Sri Ranganathaswamy") in Srirangapatna, in the Mandya district of Karnataka state, India, is dedicated to the Hindu god Ranganatha (a manifestation of the god Vishnu).

It is one of the five important pilgrimage sites of Sri Vaishnavism along the river Kaveri for devotees of Ranganatha.

These five sacred sites are together known as Pancharanga Kshetrams in Southern India.

Since Srirangapatna is the first temple starting from upstream, the deity is known as Adi Ranga .

The town of Srirangapatna, which derives its name from the temple, is located on an island in the river Kaveri.

Temple plan

The temple has an imposing tower over the entrance gate (gopura) and two large concentric rectangular enclosures (prakara) around its perimeter.

The entrance to the inner sanctum (garbhagriha) is through multiple columned halls (mantapa).

A vestibule (sukhanasi), hall (navaranga or just mantapa) and a front hall (mukhamantapa) are the other main structures in the temple.

The roof of the mukhamantapa is decorated with a "garland" ("hara") of miniature decorative towers (called "kudu" and "sala" shikharas) whose niches contain stucco images of the god Vishnu.
